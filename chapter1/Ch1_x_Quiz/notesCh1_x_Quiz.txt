All review in the summary.

1) What is the difference between initialization and assignment?
Initialization gives an initial value to a variable at the time of its creation.
Assignment gives a value to a variable after it has already been created.

2) When does undefined behavior occur? What are the consequences of undefined behavior?
Undefined behavior occurs when executed code does something not defined by the C++ language standard.
Results vary wildly; undefined behavior can work as intended by the programmer, work inconsistently, or completely not work.
